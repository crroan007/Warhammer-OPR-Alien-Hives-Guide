Before You Start — the two rules that decide the game

1. Seizing is sticky. Get a model within 3" of a marker with no enemy within 3" → it's yours, and it stays yours after you leave, until an enemy touches it. 2. Both on a marker = a tie — nobody scores it. So the plan is simple: tag markers early, then go kill everything that could come take them back. Killing IS scoring.

  1. Step 1 — Place markers toward the middle and your side.

    WHY: You tag early and they stick, so markers you can reach turn 1 are easy to own and easy to defend the lanes into.

  2. Step 2 — Deploy monsters front-and-centre, fast units on the flanks.

    WHY: The two Rexes + Burrower are your kill-spearhead; Soul-Snatchers and the Snatcher Lord are out wide to sprint onto markers turn 1.

  3. Step 3 — Try to go first.

    WHY: First turn lets you tag markers and close the distance before he can react or screen.

Round 1 — TAG & CHARGE

Grab ground and commit the spearhead. You alternate activating one unit each.

  1. Step 1 — Sprint Soul-Snatchers + Snatcher Lord onto the nearest markers and SEIZE them.

    WHY: Within 3", no enemy near = yours. Control sticks, so tag early and you can leave them later to go kill.

  2. Step 2 — Rush both Rexes (Toxico leading) straight at his army.

    WHY: Monsters are the kill-engine. Toxico's Regeneration soaks the opening fire so it arrives healthy.

  3. Step 3 — Mortar Beast holds the backfield and picks a target; Burrower stays in reserve.

    WHY: The Mortar guns down anything moving toward your new markers; the Burrower waits to drop where it hurts most.

  4. Step 4 — Do NOT camp the markers you tagged.

    WHY: They're already yours and stay yours. Your units' job now is to advance and kill, not babysit ground.

Round 2 — HIT & KILL

Melee is where you win. The charger strikes first — make it count.

  1. Step 1 — Charge the Rexes into his front line and delete a unit.

    WHY: Every unit you kill is one that can never come reclaim your markers. 13–14 attacks each wipe most squads.

  2. Step 2 — Burrower ambushes (round 2+, >9" away) behind his lines onto his most MOBILE unit or best gun.

    WHY: Bikes, transports, ambushers and fast infantry are what can reach your ground — kill those first.

  3. Step 3 — Soul-Snatchers pop a transport or charge a unit threatening a marker.

    WHY: Defend your ground by killing the things that could touch it — not by standing on it.

  4. Step 4 — Re-seize any marker he touched: kill his unit there, then step on with no enemy near.

    WHY: A contested marker is a tie. Clear him off, then you own it again.

Round 3 — GRIND HIM DOWN

Keep the pressure on. Fatigue has reset, so your beasts hit at full effect again.

  1. Step 1 — Keep the monsters killing — re-charge fresh units.

    WHY: The more of his army is dead by round 4, the fewer markers he can possibly contest.

  2. Step 2 — Hunt his remaining mobile / scoring units with the fast units + Burrower.

    WHY: Anything that can still reach a marker is your priority target this round.

  3. Step 3 — Count markers. Mark which you own and which he's standing on.

    WHY: Plan round 4 now — decide which of his markers you can clear and which of yours you must defend.

Round 4 — OWN THE GROUND

The final snapshot. Whoever alone controls the most markers wins. A shared marker scores nobody.

  1. Step 1 — For each marker you want: YOUR model within 3", NO enemy within 3".

    WHY: You only score a marker if you're alone on it. Being "near" a contested one is a wasted tie.

  2. Step 2 — If he's on a marker you need, KILL or push him off it, then step on.

    WHY: Don't settle for a tie when you can own it. Your monsters can clear almost anything off a point.

  3. Step 3 — Defend your owned markers — kill anything of his that gets within 3".

    WHY: One enemy model within 3" downgrades your owned marker to a tie. Don't let it happen.

  4. Step 4 — Re-tag any sticky marker he flipped earlier.

    WHY: If he touched one you'd left, it went neutral — step back on it (alone) to reclaim it.

  5. Step 5 — Final walk: confirm you're ALONE within 3" of every marker you're counting.

    WHY: The game is decided by inches right now. Most solely-controlled markers = you win.

Glossary — Plain Definitions

Activation
Your turn to act with one unit. Players alternate one unit at a time until all units have acted that round.
Advance (Action)
Move up to 6" (Fast 8"), then you may shoot. The all-purpose "move and still attack" action.
Ambush
The unit starts off the table and arrives on a later round, placed over 9" from enemies.
AP(X)
Armor Piercing. Lowers the defender's save by X, so they must roll X higher to save.
Artillery
A heavy platform firing powerful, often long-range/Indirect shots. A priority kill — it stops the big hits.
Blast(X)
Area attack scoring up to X hits, but capped at the number of models in the target. Your single-model Carnivo-Rex caps any Blast to just 1 hit.
Caster
A hero who spends tokens to cast spells (buffs/damage). Treat enemy casters as support to shut down.
Charge (Action)
Move up to 12" (Fast 16") into base contact to start melee. The charger STRIKES FIRST.
Deadly(X)
Each wound that gets through deals X wounds. Brutal vs multi-wound (Tough) models.
Defense
The number a defender needs to roll (or higher) to save. AP lowers it. 6 always saves, 1 always fails. Lower Defense = better.
Fast
Moves farther: +2" Advance, +4" Rush/Charge. Your whole army is Fast (except the Rex).
Fatigue
After charging or striking back, a unit only hits on natural 6s in melee for the rest of that round. Resets next round.
Fear(X)
Counts as causing X extra wounds when deciding who won a melee.
Fearless
Much better at morale; rarely flinches. Your Carnivo-Rex have this.
Flying
Ignores terrain and models while moving, in a straight line over obstacles.
Hero
A single powerful model (your Snatcher Lord) that can lead a unit or score on its own.
Hive Bond (your army rule)
Your Alien Hives faction rule. Verify its exact effect in the Army Forge ⓘ tooltip before the game and play it as written.
Hold (Action)
Move 0" and shoot. For when you're already in position.
Hold the Line (enemy rule)
The HDF does NOT rout from morale. You can't scare them off — kill them or contest by standing within 3".
Indirect
Can fire at targets it can't see (lobs over cover/terrain). Often on Artillery.
Marker / Objective
A point you score by controlling: a model within 3" while no enemy is within 3". Most markers at end of Round 4 wins.
Quality
The number a model needs to roll (or higher) to hit. Q3 hits on 3+, Q4 on 4+. Also used for morale tests.
Regeneration
After failing a save, a second roll to ignore the wound (a "heal" save).
Relentless
When the unit Holds and shoots, 6s score an extra hit.
Reliable
Always hits on 2+ regardless of Quality.
Rending
Natural 6 to hit gains big AP (AP5) and ignores Regeneration. Your Soul-Snatchers and Lord have this — great vs tanks.
Rush (Action)
Move up to 12" (Fast 16"), no shooting. Fastest way to cross the table or dive onto a marker.
Scout
Deploys later and farther forward — a head start up the table.
Shaken
Result of failing morale: idle next activation, and can't hold or contest objectives until recovered.
Strider
Ignores difficult-terrain penalties — full speed through woods/rubble. Your whole army has this.
Tough(X)
Has X wounds; only dies after taking X total. Carnivo-Rex are Tough 12.

Pick Two Goals

Doctrine reminder: markers you tag stay yours after you leave (until he touches them), and both-on-a-marker is a TIE. So contesting only denies him a point — to actually win a marker you must clear him off and stand on it alone. Default to aggression: seize early, then kill what could reclaim your ground.

About these goals — read me first. One Page Rules Grimdark Future v3.5.1 ships six official missions (Duel, Seize Ground, Relic Hunt, Sabotage, Breakthrough, King of the Hill), all resolved by controlling objective markers over 4 rounds — a unit within 3″ of a marker with no enemies within 3″ seizes it, it stays seized after you leave, and a single enemy model within 3″ contests it. The goals below are a hybrid: those marked OFFICIAL map directly onto OPR's published win conditions; those marked STRATEGIC are practical battlefield goals layered on top of the standard marker mission — how you choose to win it, not separate sanctioned scenarios. Select any two to see the combined game-plan for Monster Mash vs the HDF gunline.

  • Control the Most Markers OFFICIAL
    The default win: hold more markers than the HDF when round 4 ends.
  • Hold the Center OFFICIAL
    King-of-the-Hill: dominate one central marker and refuse to be shifted off it.
  • Break Into Enemy Ground OFFICIAL
    Breakthrough/Sabotage: push fast units deep to seize or destroy the marker the HDF camps in its backfield.
  • Assassinate Key Units STRATEGIC
    Hunt the HDF's force-multipliers — artillery, AP-heavy gun teams, heroes/casters.
  • Deny & Contest STRATEGIC
    Use speed to put one model within 3″ of enemy-held markers late, neutralizing their score.
  • Survive & Out-Point STRATEGIC
    Keep your low-model army alive — tank fire on the beasts, trade nothing free, win on objectives.

The Game Plan — Seize early, then KILL to keep

Two rules drive everything: (1) tag a marker with no enemy within 3" and it's yours — and stays yours after you leave, until an enemy touches it. (2) If both of you are within 3", it's a tie — nobody scores it. So don't camp and don't settle for contesting: seize early, then destroy everything that could reclaim your ground. Killing IS scoring — a dead unit can't take a marker.

  • Round 1 — Tag & charge. Soul-Snatchers + Snatcher Lord sprint out and seize the nearest markers (they stick). The Rexes barrel at his army; Toxico leads, Regen soaks the fire.
  • Rounds 2–3 — Kill everything. Drive the monsters in and grind. Burrower ambushes the unit most able to reach your markers. Priority kill: his fast/mobile units (bikes, transports, ambushers) that could circle back.
  • Round 4 — Own outright. Every marker you want needs YOUR model within 3" and NO enemy within 3". If he's on one you need, kill or push him off — don't accept a tie. Re-tag anything he flipped.

Offense is defense: the more of his army is dead, the fewer markers he can ever take. Tag, then tank in and kill.

What this is

OPR ships free Solo & Co-Op Rules that run an enemy army with a simple paper-and-dice “AI”, so you (or you + friends) play standard Grimdark Future against the game itself. You still play GF normally — these rules only decide what the unowned army does. v3.5 is current; a v3.6 closed beta is running (May 2026), so expect tweaks.

Setup

  • Build 3–6 balanced lists, then randomly assign which the AI runs — so you don't know exactly what you'll face.
  • Classify every AI unit (this picks its tree): Melee = no ranged weapons · Shooting = ranged better than melee · Hybrid = melee better than ranged.
  • Deploy the AI: split its units into 3 equal groups; divide its table edge into 3 sections; roll a D3 per group for its section; place units one at a time as close as possible to the nearest objective, outside difficult/dangerous terrain.

Each AI activation

Roll a D3 for the section (1–3). If that section has no eligible units, go clockwise to the next that does. Then roll for a random unit there and run its decision tree. (Shaken units activate last and just recover; Counter units activate after their section's others.)

AI Decision Trees

All three are objective-greedy first. From Solo & Co-Op v3.5.

Shooting unit

  1. Objective you don't control? → Advance toward it and shoot if that keeps an enemy in range; otherwise Rush to it.
  2. No free objective? → Advance toward the nearest enemy and shoot if you'll be in range; otherwise Rush at them.

Always tries to stay at maximum range from enemies.

Hybrid unit

  1. Free objective + enemy in the way? → Charge it if able; else Advance to the objective and shoot; else Rush to it.
  2. Free objective only in Rush range? → Rush to it.
  3. Free objective, none in the way? → Advance + shoot if it keeps an enemy in range; else Rush.
  4. No free objective? → Charge an enemy in charge range; else Advance + shoot the nearest enemy; else Rush at them.

Melee unit

  1. Free objective? → Charge any enemy in the way if able; otherwise Rush to the objective.
  2. No free objective? → Charge an enemy in charge range; otherwise Rush at the nearest enemy.

“In the way” = within 6" of the straight path to the objective. The AI controls an objective if it holds it, or has more non-Shaken units than you within 3".

Targeting Priority

Base rule: shoot/charge the nearest valid target, preferring units that haven't activated yet, and a target in the open over one in cover. Always strikes back when charged.

Special Rules & Difficulty

Challenge Bonus (difficulty dial)
Start of a round: if the AI holds as many objectives as you → all its units get +1 to hit that round. If it holds fewer → +1 to hit and +1 Defense. The community treats this as default-on, since the base AI is beatable.
Casters
Cast after moving, before attacking. Roll D3 + caster level for a random spell; if there's no legal target, cycle to the next, or skip.
Terrain
Moves into/behind cover unless rushing an objective through difficult terrain. Avoids difficult/dangerous terrain unless an objective is inside or it ignores the terrain. Flying/Strider treat it as open.
Ambush / Scout
Ambush units stay in reserve and arrive at the start of round 2, >9" from enemies. Scout units deploy last.
Artillery / Indirect / Relentless
If in range of enemies, they Hold and shoot rather than advancing. Artillery deploys on the highest line-of-sight position.
Win conditions
The Solo rules add none of their own — use whatever standard GF mission you picked. (Horde Mode is the separate wave-survival variant: 2000-pt defenders, the enemy army respawns at rounds 4/7/10, survive to round 12.)

Optional house rule — “mirror the dice”

Community fix for the AI being predictable: randomize your own activations too — roll for which of your units acts each time, the same way the AI does. Removes the human's planning edge and tightens the game. Not official.
