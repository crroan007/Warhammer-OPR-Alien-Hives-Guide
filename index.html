Before You Start (Pre-Game)

Do these three things before Round 1. Get them right and the game is half-won — your army is built to cross the table and grab ground, not to win a shooting match.

  1. Step 1 — Place objective markers where YOU can reach them fast.

    WHY: You and your opponent take turns putting down the D3+2 markers (3, 4, or 5 of them). Your whole army is Fast and runs across the board; the Human gunline wants to sit still and shoot. Place markers toward the middle and the open lanes where your beasts and Soul-Snatchers can sprint onto them. You win by controlling markers at the end of Round 4 — put them where your legs give you the advantage.

  2. Step 2 — Deploy beasts in front, fast units on the flanks.

    WHY: Put your 2 Carnivo-Rex up front and centered. They are Tough 12 and each caps any enemy Blast weapon to a single hit, so they are the cheapest place for the enemy to waste their big guns. Behind/beside them, place Soul-Snatchers, Ravenous Beasts, and the Snatcher Lord on the flanks — using the Rex as a moving wall while they sweep around toward the enemy's artillery and support.

  3. Step 3 — Try to go first.

    WHY: Going first lets you start closing immediately, before the gunline gets a full round of long-range shooting. Every inch you move turn 1 is an inch they can't shoot you crossing twice.

Round 1 — Cross the Gap

Goal: nobody fights yet. You are closing distance and putting the right bodies in front of the enemy's guns. Remember you ALTERNATE — one unit each, back and forth.

  1. Step 1 — Rush both Carnivo-Rex straight up the middle (12" each, no shooting).

    WHY: Rush is the fastest way forward and the Rex have no guns worth using. They're Tough 12 and Blast-capped to 1 hit — the safest thing to put in the open. Let the enemy waste heavy weapons on them.

  2. Step 2 — Advance your Soul-Snatchers (6"+2" Fast = 8") staying behind the Rex or terrain.

    WHY: They're your main scorers/tank-chewers but only Defense 4 — keep them shielded so they arrive alive.

  3. Step 3 — Rush the Ravenous Beasts up a flank (12"+4" Fast = 16").

    WHY: 16" gets them most of the way across. Send them down a side lane toward objectives and softer support, away from the densest firepower.

  4. Step 4 — Rush the Snatcher Lord up the opposite flank (16"), using cover.

    WHY: He's your objective-runner. Push him wide and forward, near terrain so he isn't an easy single-model kill.

  5. Step 5 — End the round. Do not charge yet.

    WHY: Charging from too far fails and leaves you exposed. Spend Round 1 entirely on positioning; the fight starts next round.

Round 2 — Hit the Line

Goal: slam your beasts into the enemy front line while your fast units race past it to the guns behind. Melee is where your army wins.

  1. Step 1 — Charge your first Carnivo-Rex into the nearest enemy unit.

    WHY: The charger STRIKES FIRST — 13 AP1 attacks on 4+ before they hit back. Charging also pins that unit so it can't keep shooting your fast units.

  2. Step 2 — Charge your second Carnivo-Rex into a different enemy unit.

    WHY: Two charges = two pinned, battered units. Spread them to lock down as much of the gunline as possible. The Rex are Fearless — they won't flinch.

  3. Step 3 — Charge the Ravenous Beasts into an enemy support/backfield unit (16" charge).

    WHY: Their 16" charge reaches past the front line. Hitting a gun crew or buff unit shuts off help to the main line.

  4. Step 4 — Rush or Charge the Soul-Snatchers toward the enemy artillery/heavy weapons.

    WHY: Rending claws chew artillery and tanks. Reach the guns if you can; if not, Rush 16" to be in their face next round. Killing artillery stops the shots that actually threaten you.

  5. Step 5 — Rush the Snatcher Lord toward the closest marker or an exposed support model (16").

    WHY: He's your runner — keep him heading where you'll need a body on a marker. A lone support model in reach dies to his 4 Rending attacks.

  6. Step 6 — Note which units charged/struck — they are now FATIGUED.

    WHY: After charging or striking back, a unit only hits on natural 6s in melee for the rest of THIS round. Don't expect a second melee from them; fatigue resets at the start of Round 3.

Round 3 — Grind and Set Up

Goal: finish the dangerous support, keep tanks busy, start drifting bodies toward markers. Fatigue has reset — beasts hit normally again.

  1. Step 1 — Finish the enemy artillery/support web with your Soul-Snatchers.

    WHY: Artillery and support (indirect guns, buffers) are what make a gunline dangerous. Kill them first, before they fire again.

  2. Step 2 — Keep your Carnivo-Rex in melee — re-charge a fresh unit or keep grinding.

    WHY: A Rex locked in melee can't be shot effectively and stops that enemy from shooting too. 13 AP1 attacks at full effect grind tanks and chew infantry — a roadblock they can't ignore.

  3. Step 3 — Peel the Ravenous Beasts toward the nearest marker if their fight is won.

    WHY: Fast and expendable enough to switch from fighting to scoring. Get a body within 3" of a marker now. This is NOT a kill game.

  4. Step 4 — Position the Snatcher Lord adjacent to (not yet on) a marker you'll hold.

    WHY: A lone hero on a marker gets sniped. Hover one move away, then step on at the last moment when there's no time for the enemy to react.

  5. Step 5 — Count markers; decide which 2–3 you can realistically hold for Round 4.

    WHY: You win by controlling the MOST markers at the end — not by tabling them. Aim every surviving unit at your reachable markers next turn.

Round 4 — Grab the Ground (STOP Fighting)

Goal: scoring only. Most markers controlled when this round ends wins. Chasing a kill you don't need can lose you the game.

  1. Step 1 — Move Soul-Snatchers ONTO an open marker (within 3", no enemy within 3").

    WHY: 5 models is a strong, hard-to-clear claim. Use Rush (16") for distance — you don't need to shoot, you need to stand on the ground.

  2. Step 2 — Move Ravenous Beasts and Snatcher Lord onto SEPARATE markers.

    WHY: Three markers with three units beats piling onto one. Each controlled marker is a point toward the win.

  3. Step 3 — If an enemy "no-rout" blob sits on a marker, move within 3" to CONTEST — don't try to kill it.

    WHY: HDF "Hold the Line" = they never run, so you can't clear them in one round. But control needs NO enemy within 3" — your model within 3" cancels their point. Contesting is faster and surer than killing.

  4. Step 4 — Use your Carnivo-Rex to body-block a marker or screen your scorers.

    WHY: A Rex within 3" of a marker contests it and is nearly impossible to shift; even when it can't "control" with an enemy near, it protects the smaller units that ARE scoring. Don't send it on a pointless kill hunt.

  5. Step 5 — Walk every marker: is one of MY models within 3" AND no enemy within 3"? Fix near-misses with your last activations.

    WHY: A model 3.5" away controls nothing. The game is decided by inches right now — nudge units fully on, contest theirs. Most markers = you win.

Glossary — Plain Definitions

Activation
Your turn to act with one unit. Players alternate one unit at a time until all units have acted that round.
Advance (Action)
Move up to 6" (Fast 8"), then you may shoot. The all-purpose "move and still attack" action.
Ambush
The unit starts off the table and arrives on a later round, placed over 9" from enemies.
AP(X)
Armor Piercing. Lowers the defender's save by X, so they must roll X higher to save.
Artillery
A heavy platform firing powerful, often long-range/Indirect shots. A priority kill — it stops the big hits.
Blast(X)
Area attack scoring up to X hits, but capped at the number of models in the target. Your single-model Carnivo-Rex caps any Blast to just 1 hit.
Caster
A hero who spends tokens to cast spells (buffs/damage). Treat enemy casters as support to shut down.
Charge (Action)
Move up to 12" (Fast 16") into base contact to start melee. The charger STRIKES FIRST.
Deadly(X)
Each wound that gets through deals X wounds. Brutal vs multi-wound (Tough) models.
Defense
The number a defender needs to roll (or higher) to save. AP lowers it. 6 always saves, 1 always fails. Lower Defense = better.
Fast
Moves farther: +2" Advance, +4" Rush/Charge. Your whole army is Fast (except the Rex).
Fatigue
After charging or striking back, a unit only hits on natural 6s in melee for the rest of that round. Resets next round.
Fear(X)
Counts as causing X extra wounds when deciding who won a melee.
Fearless
Much better at morale; rarely flinches. Your Carnivo-Rex have this.
Flying
Ignores terrain and models while moving, in a straight line over obstacles.
Hero
A single powerful model (your Snatcher Lord) that can lead a unit or score on its own.
Hive Bond (your army rule)
Your Alien Hives faction rule. Verify its exact effect in the Army Forge ⓘ tooltip before the game and play it as written.
Hold (Action)
Move 0" and shoot. For when you're already in position.
Hold the Line (enemy rule)
The HDF does NOT rout from morale. You can't scare them off — kill them or contest by standing within 3".
Indirect
Can fire at targets it can't see (lobs over cover/terrain). Often on Artillery.
Marker / Objective
A point you score by controlling: a model within 3" while no enemy is within 3". Most markers at end of Round 4 wins.
Quality
The number a model needs to roll (or higher) to hit. Q3 hits on 3+, Q4 on 4+. Also used for morale tests.
Regeneration
After failing a save, a second roll to ignore the wound (a "heal" save).
Relentless
When the unit Holds and shoots, 6s score an extra hit.
Reliable
Always hits on 2+ regardless of Quality.
Rending
Natural 6 to hit gains big AP (AP5) and ignores Regeneration. Your Soul-Snatchers and Lord have this — great vs tanks.
Rush (Action)
Move up to 12" (Fast 16"), no shooting. Fastest way to cross the table or dive onto a marker.
Scout
Deploys later and farther forward — a head start up the table.
Shaken
Result of failing morale: idle next activation, and can't hold or contest objectives until recovered.
Strider
Ignores difficult-terrain penalties — full speed through woods/rubble. Your whole army has this.
Tough(X)
Has X wounds; only dies after taking X total. Carnivo-Rex are Tough 12.

Pick Two Goals

About these goals — read me first. One Page Rules Grimdark Future v3.5.1 ships six official missions (Duel, Seize Ground, Relic Hunt, Sabotage, Breakthrough, King of the Hill), all resolved by controlling objective markers over 4 rounds — a unit within 3″ of a marker with no enemies within 3″ seizes it, it stays seized after you leave, and a single enemy model within 3″ contests it. The goals below are a hybrid: those marked OFFICIAL map directly onto OPR's published win conditions; those marked STRATEGIC are practical battlefield goals layered on top of the standard marker mission — how you choose to win it, not separate sanctioned scenarios. Select any two to see the combined game-plan for Monster Mash vs the HDF gunline.

  • Control the Most Markers OFFICIAL
    The default win: hold more markers than the HDF when round 4 ends.
  • Hold the Center OFFICIAL
    King-of-the-Hill: dominate one central marker and refuse to be shifted off it.
  • Break Into Enemy Ground OFFICIAL
    Breakthrough/Sabotage: push fast units deep to seize or destroy the marker the HDF camps in its backfield.
  • Assassinate Key Units STRATEGIC
    Hunt the HDF's force-multipliers — artillery, AP-heavy gun teams, heroes/casters.
  • Deny & Contest STRATEGIC
    Use speed to put one model within 3″ of enemy-held markers late, neutralizing their score.
  • Survive & Out-Point STRATEGIC
    Keep your low-model army alive — tank fire on the beasts, trade nothing free, win on objectives.

Quick primer (read this first)

Two numbers describe every model. Quality (Qua) is what you roll or beat on a d6 to hit — Qua 4 means "4, 5, or 6 hits." Defense (Def) is the number to save a hit; lower is better (Def 2 is a tank, Def 5 is squishy). Each turn a unit takes ONE action: Hold (stay, shoot), Advance (short move, shoot), Rush (far, no shoot), Charge (far, into melee). You're a melee army — mostly Rush then Charge.

You win by sitting on objective markers, not by killing everything. Neither side can make the other run away, so it's decided by who holds more markers at the end. Beasts soak the bullets; the fast units run the objectives.

What it does

Your giant monster and the centerpiece. A single-model unit with Tough(12) (eats 12 wounds). Fearless = almost never flees. AP1 subtracts 1 from enemy saves. Thirteen AP1 attacks on 4+ wipe a 10-model infantry squad in one charge.

The trick: because it's ONE model, enemy Blast weapons are wasted — Blast multiplies hits but is capped at the number of models in the target, so even a Blast(6) gun deals it 1 hit. Their big template guns are useless here.

How to use it

  • Walk it straight up the middle (Rush, then Charge). Their anti-beast guns only chip ~2–3 of its 12 wounds/turn — it survives ~4 turns of focused fire.
  • Charge the biggest infantry blob you can reach — 13 AP1 attacks delete a 10-model squad, removing a chunk of their shooting.
  • Bait their best guns (Fusion Arrays AP4 Deadly3, Laser Cannons AP3 Deadly3) — let the Rex soak those so your fragile units stay untouched.
  • Keep the two Rexes apart (different lanes) so their fire splits.
  • Don't be shy about objectives — a Rex on a marker is nearly impossible to shift.

Don't: hold it back out of fear (its job is to absorb fire in their faces), and don't clump both Rexes so one can be focused down before it arrives.

What it does

Five fast, accurate bodies — your primary scorers and one reliable tank-killer. Fast (+2 Advance/+4 Rush&Charge), Strider (ignore difficult terrain). Hitting on 3+ they almost always connect; Rending makes any natural 6 an AP5 hit that shreds even Defense-2 tanks.

How to use it

  • Race them to the markers turn one — Fast + Strider claims midfield before the Humans react.
  • Send them after tanks — 10 attacks on 3+ make enough 6s (AP5) to grind a Def-2 vehicle.
  • Use terrain — Strider means woods/ruins cost nothing; stay out of clean firing lines.
  • Charge fragile shooters — Human infantry fold in melee; win the combat, then sit on the marker.

Don't: burn them as a disposable speed-bump (too valuable as scorers + only reliable tank-hurter), and don't leave them in the open within obvious gun range (Tough 1 each).

  • Run them up a flank beside a Rex, using Fast to reach midfield objectives.
  • Throw them at infantry, not armor — 6 AP0 attacks chew Humans but bounce off tanks.
  • Use them as a durable objective anchor — Tough(3) is annoying for a low-AP gunline to clear.
  • Let them screen the Soul-Snatchers by drawing fire (they survive hits better).

Don't: send them after tanks (no Rending/AP — they achieve nothing), and don't blob all three onto one Rex in a clump.

What it does

A cheap, fast Hero — he can join a multi-model unit (Hero with Tough ≤6 may join). Hits on 3+ with 4 AP1 Rending attacks (every 6 = AP5). Fast + Strider make him a great late-game runner to sprint onto an undefended marker.

How to use it

  • Glue him to the Ravenous Beasts as a punchy leader (his Rending adds tank-threat + accuracy)…
  • …or run him solo late to grab/contest an open objective.
  • Use his speed to react — hold back, then Rush to wherever a marker is undefended on the final turns.
  • Lean on Rending — point those 4 dice at something with a good save.

Don't: park him alone in the open within sniper/clean-shot range — a single Tough(3) model worth picking off. When in doubt, tuck him in a unit so he can't be targeted alone.

Army rule & synergy

Hive Bond is your shared army rule — verify its exact effect via the Army Forge ⓘ tooltip before the game; the wording determines how your units support each other.

Synergy in one line: the two Carnivo-Rex soak the gunline's fire and delete infantry up the middle, while the Fast Soul-Snatchers, Ravenous Beasts, and Snatcher Lord flank past the distracted Humans to grab and hold the markers that win the game.
