Before You Start — the two rules that decide the game

1. Seizing is sticky. Get a model within 3" of a marker with no enemy within 3" → it's yours, and it stays yours after you leave, until an enemy touches it. 2. Both on a marker = a tie — nobody scores it. You are SLOW, so play a turtle: seize the close cluster of markers, hold them with Sturdy bodies, and use your two fast vehicles + the ambush drill to kill anything that could reach your ground. You don't chase — you make him come to you and break on your wall.

  1. Step 1 — Place markers toward the centre and YOUR side.

    WHY: You move -2"/-4" (Slow). Pull the scoring onto ground you can actually reach and hold; never bid for a marker stranded in his deployment.

  2. Step 2 — Deploy a tight castle. Load a Berserker squad INTO the Fortress Tank.

    WHY: The Fortress Tank (Fast, Transport 16) is your delivery system — it drives a Slow Berserker squad 12"+ upfield turn 1 so they can actually charge turn 2. Iron Veterans + Thunder Support anchor the centre.

  3. Step 3 — Sniper Team uses SCOUT; Assault Drill goes into reserve (Ambush).

    WHY: Snipers pre-deploy within 12" of your zone to threaten his hero from turn 1. The Drill waits to drop on his backline round 2.

  4. Step 4 — Try to go SECOND.

    WHY: A turtle wants the last activation each round to react and to grab the final-round marker count. Letting him commit first tells you where to counter-punch.

Round 1 — TAG & ROLL UP

Seize the close markers and get the wall moving. You alternate activating one unit each.

  1. Step 1 — Iron Veterans + a Berserker squad SEIZE the central / near markers.

    WHY: Within 3", no enemy near = yours, and it sticks. Sturdy (+1 Defense when shot from over 9") makes them miserable to shift.

  2. Step 2 — Fortress Tank (carrying Berserkers) + Attack Vehicle drive forward.

    WHY: These are your only Fast pieces. The Tank ferries the Berserkers into charge range; the Attack Vehicle slides to a flank to line up his tanks.

  3. Step 3 — Thunder Support + Sniper Team open fire from cover; Assault Drill stays in reserve.

    WHY: The Mortar lobs Indirect (no line-of-sight needed) at his densest blob; the Sniper's Takedown picks his hero/caster off a unit. The Drill waits.

  4. Step 4 — Do NOT over-extend the infantry.

    WHY: You're Slow and tanky, not fast and fragile. Hold the line — make him cross the table into your guns and counter-charge.

Round 2 — COUNTER-PUNCH

This is the turn the trap springs. The charger strikes first — and your axes get vicious on the charge.

  1. Step 1 — Assault Drill AMBUSHES (round 2+, >9" away) behind his lines onto his best gun / vehicle.

    WHY: A4 AP4 Tunneling Drill + Impact(6) on the charge wrecks artillery, gun teams, or a tank — the things that out-range your turtle.

  2. Step 2 — Berserkers disembark the Fortress Tank and CHARGE.

    WHY: Melee Slayer gives them AP(+2) vs Tough(3)+ targets on the charge — their Great Axes go AP2 → AP4 into his vehicles/monsters. Furious adds extra hits on 6s.

  3. Step 3 — Attack Vehicle kills a vehicle; Fortress Tank guns down whatever's closest.

    WHY: Twin Heavy Fusion (AP4, Deadly 3) deletes a transport/tank; the Tank throws 16 shots into his approaching infantry.

  4. Step 4 — Re-seize any marker he touched: clear him off, then stand on it alone.

    WHY: A contested marker is a tie. Kill the contester, own it again.

Round 3 — HOLD & GRIND

The wall holds. Fatigue has reset, so your axes and drill hit full effect again.

  1. Step 1 — Keep killing anything that can REACH your markers.

    WHY: You can't run markers down across the table — so deny him by destroying the units that could come take yours. His bikes, transports, fast infantry first.

  2. Step 2 — Berserkers + Drill re-charge fresh targets; Attack Vehicle hunts the next vehicle.

    WHY: Melee Slayer keeps your axes at AP4 into his tough stuff every time they charge. Stay on the offensive within your bubble.

  3. Step 3 — Count markers. Note which you own and which he's standing on.

    WHY: Plan round 4 now — which of his can you clear with a charge, and which of yours must you screen?

Round 4 — OWN THE GROUND

The final snapshot. Whoever alone controls the most markers wins. A shared marker scores nobody.

  1. Step 1 — For each marker you want: YOUR model within 3", NO enemy within 3".

    WHY: You only score a marker if you're alone on it. Being "near" a contested one is a wasted tie.

  2. Step 2 — If he's on a marker you need, KILL or push him off it, then step on.

    WHY: Don't settle for a tie when you can own it. A charging Berserker squad (AP4 vs Tough) or the Drill clears almost anything off a point.

  3. Step 3 — Screen your owned markers — body-block or kill anything of his that gets within 3".

    WHY: One enemy model within 3" downgrades your owned marker to a tie. Sturdy infantry standing on it is hard to remove.

  4. Step 4 — Re-tag any sticky marker he flipped earlier.

    WHY: If he touched one you'd left, it went neutral — step back on it (alone) to reclaim it.

  5. Step 5 — Final walk: confirm you're ALONE within 3" of every marker you're counting.

    WHY: The game is decided by inches right now. Most solely-controlled markers = you win.

Glossary — Plain Definitions

Activation
Your turn to act with one unit. Players alternate one unit at a time until all units have acted that round.
Advance (Action)
Move up to 6" (Slow 4"), then you may shoot. The all-purpose "move and still attack" action.
Ambush
The unit starts off the table and arrives from round 2+, placed over 9" from enemies. Can't seize/contest the round it arrives. Your Assault Drill has this.
AP(X)
Armor Piercing. Targets get −X to their Defense rolls, so they must roll X higher to save.
Artillery
A heavy platform firing powerful, often long-range/Indirect shots. A priority kill — it stops the big hits. Your Sniper's Takedown or the Drill's ambush handle these.
Blast(X)
Ignores cover; each hit is multiplied by X, capped at the number of models in the target. Your Thunder Support Mortar uses Blast(3) — great vs a horde, wasted on a single big model.
Caster
A hero who spends tokens to cast spells (buffs/damage). Treat enemy casters as support to shut down — Sniper Takedown is your answer.
Charge (Action)
Move up to 12" (Slow 8") into base contact to start melee. The charger STRIKES FIRST. This is when Melee Slayer and Furious switch on.
Deadly(X)
Each wound assigned to a model is multiplied by X (resolved first; doesn't carry over). Your Attack Vehicle's Fusion is AP4 Deadly(3) — brutal vs Tough models/vehicles.
Defense
The number a defender needs to roll (or higher) to save. AP lowers it. A natural 6 always saves, 1 always fails. Lower Defense = better.
Fast
Moves farther: +2" Advance, +4" Rush/Charge. Only your three vehicles are Fast — the infantry is Slow.
Fatigue
After charging or striking back, a unit only hits on natural 6s in melee for the rest of that round. Resets next round.
Fear(X)
Counts as causing X extra wounds when deciding who won a melee.
Fearless
Failed morale → roll a die, 4+ counts as passed. Your Berserkers have this — they don't flinch.
Fortified
Incoming hits count as AP(−1) (min AP 0) — softens enemy armor-piercing. Your Dwarf Champion carries this.
Furious
When charging, unmodified 6s to hit in melee deal 1 extra hit. Your Berserkers — more axes land on the charge.
Hero
A single powerful model (Berserker Veteran / Dwarf Champion) that joins a unit, leads its morale, and adds attacks.
Hold (Action)
Move 0" and shoot. Your turtle's bread-and-butter — Thunder Support and Iron Veterans want to Hold.
Impact(X)
When this model charges (and isn't fatigued), roll X dice; each 2+ is an automatic hit before melee. Fortress Tank Impact(9), Assault Drill Impact(6).
Indirect
Can fire at targets it can't see (lobs over cover/terrain), ignores cover; −1 to hit if it moved. Your Mortar.
Marker / Objective
A point you score by controlling: a model within 3" while no enemy is within 3". Most markers at end of Round 4 wins.
Melee Slayer
When this model charges, its weapons get AP(+2) if most of the target has Tough(3)+. Your Berserkers' Great Axes hit AP4 into vehicles/monsters on the charge.
Quality
The number a model needs to roll (or higher) to hit. Q3 hits on 3+, Q4 on 4+. Also used for morale tests.
Reliable
Always hits on 2+ regardless of Quality. Your Sniper Rifles and the Drill's Heavy Flamer.
Rush (Action)
Move up to 12" (Slow 8"), no shooting. Your Fast vehicles cover ground here; your Slow infantry barely moves.
Scout
Deploys after all other units, anywhere fully within 12" of your zone — a head start. Your Sniper Team has this.
Shaken
Result of failing morale: idle next activation, and can't hold or contest objectives until recovered.
Shred
On an enemy save roll of natural 1, deal 1 extra wound.
Slow
Moves less: −2" Advance, −4" Rush/Charge. Almost your whole infantry — this is WHY you turtle and why the Fortress Tank ferries Berserkers.
Stealth
When shot from over 9" away, enemies get −1 to hit. Your Sniper Team survives in the open.
Strider
Ignores difficult-terrain movement penalties. Your Attack Vehicle has this.
Sturdy (army-wide)
When shot or charged from over 9" away, the unit gets +1 to Defense rolls. Your whole army shrugs off ranged fire and long charges — the core of the turtle.
Surge
On unmodified 6s to hit, the weapon deals 1 extra hit. Fortress Tank Heavy C-Beamer.
Takedown
The shooter picks ANY single model in the target (resolved as a unit of 1) — sniper a hero out of a squad. Your Sniper Team.
Tough(X)
Has X wounds; only dies after taking X total. Fortress Tank is Tough 18; the Drill Tough 12.
Transport(X)
Carries up to X model-spaces. Fortress Tank Transport(16) ferries a Slow Berserker squad into charge range turn 1.

Doctrine reminder: markers you tag stay yours after you leave (until he touches them), and both-on-a-marker is a TIE. You are Slow + Sturdy, so play the turtle: seize the close cluster, hold it on tanky bodies, and counter-punch with the two fast vehicles + the ambush Drill to kill anything that could reach your ground. You don't chase — he breaks on your wall.

About these goals — read me first. One Page Rules Grimdark Future v3.5.1 ships six official missions (Duel, Seize Ground, Relic Hunt, Sabotage, Breakthrough, King of the Hill), all resolved by controlling objective markers over 4 rounds — a unit within 3″ of a marker with no enemies within 3″ seizes it, it stays seized after you leave, and a single enemy model within 3″ contests it. The goals below are a hybrid: those marked OFFICIAL map directly onto OPR's published win conditions; those marked STRATEGIC are practical battlefield goals layered on top of the standard marker mission — how you choose to win it, not separate sanctioned scenarios.   • Control the Most Markers OFFICIAL
    The default win: hold more markers than Bill when round 4 ends.
  • Hold the Center OFFICIAL
    King-of-the-Hill: plant the Fortress Tank + Sturdy infantry on one central marker and refuse to be shifted.
  • Break Into Enemy Ground OFFICIAL
    Breakthrough/Sabotage: the Assault Drill ambushes deep to seize or wreck the marker he camps in his backfield.
  • Assassinate Key Units STRATEGIC
    Hunt his force-multipliers — artillery, AP-heavy gun teams, heroes/casters — with the Sniper's Takedown and the Drill.
  • Deny & Contest STRATEGIC
    Body-block or kill anything that steps within 3″ of your held markers; turn his charge into a tie, then clear it.
  • Survive & Out-Point STRATEGIC
    Lean on Sturdy + Tough; soak his shooting, trade nothing free, win on the marker count.

The Game Plan — Turtle, then counter-punch

Two rules drive everything: (1) tag a marker with no enemy within 3" and it's yours — and stays yours after you leave, until an enemy touches it. (2) If both of you are within 3", it's a tie — nobody scores it. You're Slow, so you can't chase markers across the table: seize the close cluster, hold it with Sturdy bodies, and kill anything that could come reclaim it.

  • Round 1 — Tag & roll up. Iron Veterans + a Berserker squad seize the near/central markers (Sturdy makes them hard to shift). Fortress Tank (carrying the other Berserkers) + Attack Vehicle drive forward; Mortar & Sniper open fire; Drill in reserve.
  • Round 2 — Counter-punch. Assault Drill ambushes his best gun/vehicle (AP4 + Impact 6). Berserkers disembark and charge — Melee Slayer turns their axes to AP4 vs Tough(3)+. Attack Vehicle kills a tank.
  • Rounds 3–4 — Hold & own. Keep destroying whatever can reach your markers. Round 4: every marker you want needs YOUR model within 3" and NO enemy within 3". Clear contesters with a charge; re-tag anything he flipped.

Defense is offense: he has to cross the table into your Sturdy wall and counter-charges. Don't over-extend — make him break on you.

🎙️ Solo / Co-Op Voice Coach

Hands full of dice? Tap the mic and ask. It runs OPR's Solo & Co-Op AI rules (v3.5) entirely on your device — no internet call, no account, nothing typed. Whatever it says is also printed below, and every question is a tappable button too.

Tap, ask, done — e.g. “hybrid unit”, “how does it pick targets”, or “roll activation”

What this is

OPR ships free Solo & Co-Op Rules that run an enemy army with a simple paper-and-dice “AI”, so you (or you + friends) play standard Grimdark Future against the game itself. You still play GF normally — these rules only decide what the unowned army does. v3.5 is current; a v3.6 closed beta is running (May 2026), so expect tweaks.

Setup

  • Build 3–6 balanced lists, then randomly assign which the AI runs — so you don't know exactly what you'll face.
  • Classify every AI unit (this picks its tree): Melee = no ranged weapons · Shooting = ranged better than melee · Hybrid = melee better than ranged.
  • Deploy the AI: split its units into 3 equal groups; divide its table edge into 3 sections; roll a D3 per group for its section; place units one at a time as close as possible to the nearest objective, outside difficult/dangerous terrain.

Each AI activation

Roll a D3 for the section (1–3). If that section has no eligible units, go clockwise to the next that does. Then roll for a random unit there and run its decision tree. (Shaken units activate last and just recover; Counter units activate after their section's others.)

AI Decision Trees

All three are objective-greedy first. From Solo & Co-Op v3.5.

Shooting unit

  1. Objective you don't control? → Advance toward it and shoot if that keeps an enemy in range; otherwise Rush to it.
  2. No free objective? → Advance toward the nearest enemy and shoot if you'll be in range; otherwise Rush at them.

Always tries to stay at maximum range from enemies.

Hybrid unit

  1. Free objective + enemy in the way? → Charge it if able; else Advance to the objective and shoot; else Rush to it.
  2. Free objective only in Rush range? → Rush to it.
  3. Free objective, none in the way? → Advance + shoot if it keeps an enemy in range; else Rush.
  4. No free objective? → Charge an enemy in charge range; else Advance + shoot the nearest enemy; else Rush at them.

Melee unit

  1. Free objective? → Charge any enemy in the way if able; otherwise Rush to the objective.
  2. No free objective? → Charge an enemy in charge range; otherwise Rush at the nearest enemy.

“In the way” = within 6" of the straight path to the objective. The AI controls an objective if it holds it, or has more non-Shaken units than you within 3".

Targeting Priority

Base rule: shoot/charge the nearest valid target, preferring units that haven't activated yet, and a target in the open over one in cover. Always strikes back when charged.

Special Rules & Difficulty

Challenge Bonus (difficulty dial)
Start of a round: if the AI holds as many objectives as you → all its units get +1 to hit that round. If it holds fewer → +1 to hit and +1 Defense. The community treats this as default-on, since the base AI is beatable.
Casters
Cast after moving, before attacking. Roll D3 + caster level for a random spell; if there's no legal target, cycle to the next, or skip.
Terrain
Moves into/behind cover unless rushing an objective through difficult terrain. Avoids difficult/dangerous terrain unless an objective is inside or it ignores the terrain. Flying/Strider treat it as open.
Ambush / Scout
Ambush units stay in reserve and arrive at the start of round 2, >9" from enemies. Scout units deploy last.
Artillery / Indirect / Relentless
If in range of enemies, they Hold and shoot rather than advancing. Artillery deploys on the highest line-of-sight position.
Win conditions
The Solo rules add none of their own — use whatever standard GF mission you picked. (Horde Mode is the separate wave-survival variant: 2000-pt defenders, the enemy army respawns at rounds 4/7/10, survive to round 12.)

Optional house rule — “mirror the dice”

Community fix for the AI being predictable: randomize your own activations too — roll for which of your units acts each time, the same way the AI does. Removes the human's planning edge and tightens the game. Not official.
